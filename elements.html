Podcasts

In this section you will find my favourite podcasts related to Data Analytics and other current topics that interest me.

Talk on LLM in Data Analytics with CTO of Microsoft Fabric

I recently listened to an episode of the 'Data Skeptic' podcast—'LLMs for Data Analysis'—and it was very interesting. Amir Netz from Microsoft talked about the progression of business intelligence tools and the latest Microsoft offerings like Power BI and Fabric. He explains how Fabric sets itself apart with its end-to-end approach and the seamless integration of machine learning models. The episode also touches on future updates and practical ways to ensure reliable outputs from tools like Copilot. It’s a great listen for anyone into tech and data analytics, and it's given me some interesting insights for my work in product management.

Gartner ThinkCast On the Symbiosis of Humans and AI in Data Analytics

I checked out a Gartner ThinkCast episode called 'How Data and Analytics Can Bring AI and Humans Together' and the ideas mentioned in it stuck with me. It was a great listen, discussing the importance of trustworthy data and analytics in AI decision-making. The speakers, Debra Logan and Anitha Zaidi, really drilled into how embedding AI capabilities into an organization's DNA goes beyond just tech adoption—it's about shaping data governance and driving business value. They shared some interesting points on steering organizations towards success with AI. Definitely gave me a lot to think about in terms of using AI responsibly in product management.

The Lean Startup Apprach in Data Analytics

Just tuned into an episode of 'Driven by Data: The Podcast'—'The Lean StartUp Approach to Data & Analytics with Ben Dias'. It was an engaging listen, covering how the lean startup methodology applies to data science and analytics at easyJet. Ben Dias talks about tackling unique problems through innovative algorithms, personal development, and taking a lean approach in building a data-driven foundation. The conversation included insights on the value of a flat organizational structure and the importance of a diverse team. It's a great episode that combines the best of business strategy and data, and it's given me some actionable ideas for team collaboration and data strategy, particularly for my own startup.

Videos

In this section you will find highly recent and varied videos and talks from inspiring, versatile people talking about Data Analytics and other subjects I find relevant.

Beyond the Data - A Data Analyst Journey

I recently watched an insightful YouTube video about the importance of investing time in people, processes, and resources when conducting data analysis. The video emphasized that understanding the context of data leads to more effective analysis and outcomes. It discussed the importance of a clear and neutral mindset in conducting thorough research, considering both the larger context and finer details, acknowledging biases, and identifying the purpose of the analysis. The presenter, a multifaceted professional juggling roles in data analysis, instructional design, and family life, shared experiences from a varied career and education, demonstrating that data analytics is as inclusive as it is multidisciplinary. I definitely took some insights from this video that I can apply to my own career.

Data Visualizations

After watching Dustin Cabral's engaging YouTube talk, I was once again made aware of the profound impact data visualization has across a spectrum of industries. As a consultant at Cleartelligence and a seasoned Bryant University graduate, Dustin showcases data visualization as an essential tool that transcends boundaries, from retail to healthcare. His compelling demonstration of transforming complex data into clear, insightful visuals has reinforced my appreciation for the art of data storytelling. His dedication to educating others inspires me to further integrate these tools in my work to uncover hidden insights and inform decision-making.

Data Analytics and the Environment

Reflecting on Dan Hammer's talk on the use of technology for environmental protection was really eye-opening to watch. His approach to combining data science and satellite imagery to monitor global forests demonstrates the powerful role of tech in sustainable practices. With his role that blends technology and environmental policy, Dan embodies the interdisciplinary spirit that drives modern conservation efforts and has opened up some new perspiectives for my personal career direction. His work with Global Forest Watch exemplifies the potential to use data for greater transparency and action in environmental issues. This has deepened my understanding of how vital and impactful data can be in driving meaningful change, a lesson I aim to apply within my own sphere to contribute to a more sustainable future.

Continuous Learning

I hold the belief that continual learning and staying curious are fundamental to personal and professional growth. In a rapidly evolving business landscape, keeping up with the latest trends and technologies is not just a necessity but in my believe crucial for success. This is why I am committed to engaging in different online courses across platforms, ensuring a comprehensive and current understanding of new tools and methodologies. Here, I am sharing some courses I did in the last months.

Introduction to Tableau in DataCamp

Exploring the landscape of data visualization tools, I recently completed an introductory course on Tableau through DataCamp. This was an insightful foray into understanding how one of Power BI's main competitors approaches data analysis and visualization. The course provided hands-on experience with Tableau's interface and capabilities, sharpening my ability to interpret and portray data effectively. This strategic insight not only broadens my technical toolkit but also ensures that I remain well-versed with the diverse software options available in the market for my role in product management and business analytics.

Principles of GenAI in Kubicle

Recently, I have been studying Kubicle's 'Principles of Generative AI' courses. The courses cover a wide range of topics, from the basics of AI to its practical applications in business, such as text analysis. I found the sections on Natural Language Processing and Generative AI particularly interesting, especially when experimenting with ChatGPT. The capabilities of AI are impressive in modern times. In general, these courses provide a balanced blend of education without delving too deeply into technical aspects, so it might also be interesting to take a look at for people who are not that tech-savvy.

  • Go to Kubicle

Implementing AI Solutions in Business in DataCamp

I recently completed a course titled "Implementing AI Solutions in Business" on DataCamp, which was a deep dive into the practical applications of artificial intelligence in the business realm. This course equipped me with the knowledge to identify opportunities for AI integration within various business processes and provided a roadmap for deploying AI solutions effectively. It covered key topics such as evaluating AI readiness, understanding the AI lifecycle, and navigating the ethical considerations of AI implementation. The course also emphasized the importance of collaboration between data scientists and business stakeholders to drive AI initiatives that align with organizational goals. With these new insights, I feel more confident in my ability to guide AI projects from conception to realization, ensuring they deliver tangible value to the business.
